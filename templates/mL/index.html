In our analysis we wanted to look at what it takes to create a profitible Division 1 football program. We began our analysis by creating a simple scatter plot to prove linearity before moving on and creating the model. This is an important step in determining which kind of model to use. Profit v. Total Expenditures


Secondly, we look a look at potential profits based on total football expeditures. Again most Divison 1 schools are spending less than $60 million a year and breaking even. This was an interesting outcome because in our research most of our recognizable Power 5 Division 1 schools are spending well above 110 million to finance their programs. While they might be an anomaly when looking at all 130 Division 1 football programs, we know extra spending does not always guarantee success. While their are other factors that can impact the success of a team we believe that colleges that spend around 80 million on their football programs get the best return. The data is very inconclusive as colleges increase spending. Since high spending does not guarantee success it is hard to predict the just how much colleges should expect in return. Many things like injury or lack of attendance can easily impact profits.

Limitations


When we began our data exploration and initial modeling, we wanted to try to find the "sweet spot" for expenditure and cash inflows from various sources. We were fortunate enough to find an excellent dataset that gave us the breakdown of all major categories of inflows and outflows, and we wanted to predict where a team would be if they stayed on their current path, made a significant investment in the team, or emphasized bringing money in through various means. We created a correlation matrix to figure out which of our independent variables were most responsible for revenue. We created our model with 7 variables ranging from coach's salary and recruiting expenses to advertising revenue and ticket sales. Unfortunately we learned that making multivariate forecasts is virtually impossible unless using a time series model. It is also extremely hard to interpret the results as you can see in this least squares regression plot. After learning this, we shifted our focus to plotting potential profit as predicted by actual and predicted revenues.
