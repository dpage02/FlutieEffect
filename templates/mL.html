Total Expenses v. Total Revenue


In our analysis we wanted to look at what it takes to create a successful Division 1 football program. On our x axis we have the expenses by team and their resulting revenue on the y axis. As expected the more you send on a program the more your revenue should increase. Most teams are spending under $50 million while larger schools can afford to spend upwards up $100 to $160 million in some cases. However, after about $100 million to $110 million spent we start to see more negative returns than positive. This was an intresting outcome because in our research most of our recognizable Power 5 Division 1 schools are spending well above 110 million to finance their programs. While they might be an anomaly when looking at all 130 Division 1 football programs, we know extra spending does not always guarantee success. While their are other factors that can impact the success of a team we believe that colleges that spend around 80 million on their football programs get the best return.

Profit v. Total Expenditures


Secondly, we look a look at profits based on total football expeditures. Again most Divison 1 schools are spending less than $60 million a year and breaking even. The data is very inconclusive as colleges increase spending. Since high spending does not guarantee success it is hard to predict the just how much colleges should expect in return. Many things like injury or lack of attendance can easily impact profits.
